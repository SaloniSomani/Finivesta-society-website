DARE TO DREAM

BY JIYA

Remember the time when your eyes were shining with the thought of the future? When the future became synonymous with freedom. Childhood was the time when we realised dreaming needs no money or any degree but enthusiasm and a mind that can run wild. We were ready to be everything from the police to doctors, from stepping into business to owning a whole city. None of these sent butterflies or any knots inside the stomach. None of these made us nervous or doubt ourselves. We were ready to become whoever we want to be, whatsoever the world could offer. While growing up, even the stage starts to frighten, bosses and managers become vampires and interviewers turn into monsters who can reject you for any reason. Everything became different and while adapting to this difference we forgot what we really wanted to be. Now the person standing in front of us, in the mirror, is totally different with a different job than expected, with a different persona. Perhaps everything is better but mostly that change doesn't excite us just like dreaming used to in our childhood. Even while looking in the mirror there was a discombobulated look on the face and a mind with thousands of “what if’s”. Do we really want to be like this? To fear something that's behind a door rather than prepare for it. To doubt ourselves even when a whole lot of people believe in us. We forgot how to believe in ourselves. The future is uncertain. The people who will stand with us are uncertain. The job that we’re going to have is uncertain. And how this uncertainty will treat us is a question itself and the root of all these doubts. Even while preparing for any competitive exam or a simple presentation in front of the class we could feel butterflies in our stomachs or worse dancing feet. But what are we afraid of? It's either the outcome or the opinion. The outcome is going to impact our future and the impact can be terribly atrocious or highly satisfactory. We can think about the good part but our brain focuses on the atrocious one. It starts with what if ‘I will fail’ and this fear of failure or rejection leads to gloomy feelings and makes us feel worthless. Every time you try to escape it will turn stronger and will make you a part of it without you knowing. Anticipatory anxiety describes the fear and worries about bad things that could happen. It can happen in a lot of different contexts, but it commonly focuses on things we can't predict or control. Most people couldn't start because they are afraid of losing. The constant fear of losing causes several good ideas to go in vain. A man, let's call him Arjun, was working under one of his relatives. Where his relative was a wholesaler and Arjun was just an ordinary retailer. he learnt everything under his relative's guidance but stayed a retailer out of respect. Arjun was hardworking and wanted to live a better life with his family. So he did two jobs, earning enough to feed his wife and two children. Arjuns relatives have to move to some other part of the country and change their business too. After his relative everything was supposed to transfer to Arjun, as he was their only relative. But that didn't happen. His relative gave it to some random person. Now Arjun wanted to start his own shop but he was afraid. The fear of the outcome, whether it will work out or not. Like many of us, Arjun was afraid to start his own business, not that he was not capable or experienced. But because he was just not confident enough. So how should we stop these fears from taking us over? In the movie ‘Three Idiots', Aamir Khan taught us how to fool our brains. Try using mantras like him. Just like he used to say “ all is well”, try something for yourself. If your mind is constantly putting you in a loop of doubts then fool it. “Everything is safe, I'll do great. I've worked hard.” repeat these till you realise you're fine. Journaling is very helpful. The feeling of us being alone is removed while writing it down. Most of the time we need to put things down on a piece of paper and everything seems so simple. If you want to help yourself in the long run then nothing is better than mindfulness. Mindfulness not only calms you down but helps you in introspection. It also helps in staying calm and composed in difficult situations In the case of Arjun, his wife helped him fool his mind. She said, she’ll help him out in every situation and motivated him to move forward. From living in a small room to building his own houses, from walking in old weary slippers to owning cars he did a great job. And everything started after conquering the fear. So, it's okay if the future scares you and you find it hard to dream. In this realistic world, your dreams might look unrealistic to many. But it's always worth a try then regretting it your whole life. I remember reading a quote, “things are never as bleak as they seem in the midst of anxiety ”
